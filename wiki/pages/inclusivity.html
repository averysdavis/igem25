Obstacles

Worldwide, knowledge about synthetic biology is limited. In 2021, 85% of people were not even aware that synthetic biology existed [1]. Many in the U.S. find it difficult to access science due to a variety of factors including gender, race, age, socioeconomic status, and language barriers. We sought to overcome these obstacles in our team and our efforts

Our Team

We aspire to make synthetic biology accessible to all students. As the only high school iGEM team in all of Northern California, we understand that being based in a private school is one of many barriers to synthetic biology. To account for this, we encourage non-Khan Lab School students to join our team. As a result, our team represents a wide variety of public and private schools including Helios School, Gunn High School, La Entrada School, Proof School, Bellarmine College Preparatory, and San Francisco University High School.

We believe diversity must come from within and outside the team. Our team members represent a diverse set of different identities, with 88% of our members identifying as people of color, 53% of our members identifying as female, and collectively speaking 14 different languages.

Community Lab

Despite its name, Khan Lab School does not have laboratory space. Due to this, our team is a proud member of Biocurious, a community lab in Santa Clara. Biocurious connects us to other scientists and community efforts. This allows us to engage our community and form long-lasting connections.

Education

Our educational efforts directly brought synthetic biology to a diverse set of students. We visited both public and private schools, including Helios school (private) and Laurel Elementary (public). Our session at Laurel Elementary was run in Spanish and English, to cater to the Spanish immersion students. Our session with students from China was run >entirely in Mandarin, with discussion and Q&As between our members and the students.

Additionally, we ran a class at the Mandir Shreemaya Krishnadhan Temple, a Hindu temple in San Jose. This allowed us to reach our local Hindu community.

In collaboration with Stanford University’s Stanley Qi Lab, we helped develop CRISPR Kit curriculum to cater to high school students, an effort which is still ongoing through Khan Lab School’s cornerstone program. Our educational sessions with Qi Lab at Khan Lab School gave our students hands-on synthetic biology lab experience. Students were given the opportunity to further explore CRISPR, bioengineering, and more with mentorship from the Qi Lab.

Our team posted about our efforts on social media, spreading awareness about our team and mission, allowing us to reach anyone with an internet connection.

See the education page for more.

Wiki Accessibility

Our team designed and built our wiki to follow the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). All images are accompanied by alt tags for those with visual impairments. Alt tags make images accessible through the use of screen readers or braille converters. The color palette used is friendly to those with color blindness, with a 99.85% confidence rating [2]. All videos have verified captions, with videos not in English being captioned in the original language and English.

Future Plans

We push our own members to pursue synthetic biology even after graduation. During our joint visit with the UCSC iGEM team, visiting UCSC students encouraged our members to pursue further education. The students explained the value of not just an undergraduate education in related fields, but encouraged our members to pursue graduate school, and careers or research in synthetic biology.
